# Commercialization Options

The selected license is MIT. The project owner can charge for services and complementary products, while every recipient can also use the MIT-licensed code commercially if they preserve the required notices.

Potential revenue models:

- Paid installation, onboarding, and environment setup.
- Custom visual systems, templates, workflows, and private extensions.
- Team training, audits, support, and maintenance subscriptions.
- Hosted or managed content-production services, subject to the selected license.
- Sponsorships, donations, and paid priority support.
- Trademarked certification or an official hosted edition, while keeping platform brand names separate.

Do not base revenue projections on per-copy royalties, exclusive use of an MIT-released version, or forcing downstream users to publish their modifications. Those controls are not provided by MIT.

Do not promise revenue, platform reach, account safety, or patentability. Keep paid service contracts, tax handling, support scope, and intellectual-property ownership explicit.
